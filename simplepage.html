World Currency

U.S. Dollar (USD):

The most widely used currency for global trade and the primary reserve currency of the world. It's issued by the United States Federal Reserve.

Euro (EUR):

The official currency of the Eurozone, used by 19 of the 27 European Union countries. It is the second most traded currency in the world.

Japanese Yen (JPY):

The official currency of Japan, it is the third most traded currency and a common reserve currency.

British Pound Sterling (GBP):

The currency of the United Kingdom, it's often referred to simply as "pound" and is one of the oldest currencies still in use.

Swiss Franc (CHF):

The currency of Switzerland, known for its stability and often used as a safe haven currency in times of global uncertainty.

Chinese Yuan (CNY):

The official currency of the People's Republic of China, also known as the Renminbi (RMB). It's becoming increasingly important in global finance.

Canadian Dollar (CAD):

The currency of Canada, often called the "loonie" after the bird on the one-dollar coin.
